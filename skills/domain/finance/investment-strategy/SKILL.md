# Investment Strategy

## Core Principles
1. **Diversification**: Don't put all eggs in one basket. Spread across asset classes, sectors, and geographies.
2. **Asset Allocation**: The primary driver of returns (more than individual stock picking).
3. **Low Cost**: Fees (expense ratios) eat into returns over decades. Prefer low-cost index funds/ETFs.

## 1. Asset Classes
- **Equities (Stocks)**: High risk, high potential return. Ownership in companies.
- **Fixed Income (Bonds)**: Lower risk, provides steady income (coupons).
- **Cash Equivalents**: Very low risk, high liquidity (Money Market).
- **Alternatives**: Real Estate (REITs), Gold, Crypto, Commodities.

## 2. Risk Profiling
- **Risk Tolerance**: Emotional ability to handle market swings.
- **Risk Capacity**: Financial ability to handle losses based on timeline and wealth.
- **Timeline**: 
  - Short-term (<3 yrs): Focus on Capital Preservation (Cash, Bonds).
  - Long-term (>10 yrs): Focus on Growth (Stocks).

## 3. Allocation Models
- **Conservative**: 20% Stocks / 80% Bonds (Preservation).
- **Balanced**: 60% Stocks / 40% Bonds (The Classic 60/40).
- **Aggressive**: 90% Stocks / 10% Bonds or 100% Stocks (Max Growth).

## 4. Rebalancing
The process of bringing your portfolio back to its target allocation.
- **Why**: Prevents your portfolio from becoming too risky (e.g., stocks growing to 80% when target was 60%).
- **When**: Quarterly, Annually, or when a class drifts by >5%.

## Gotchas
- **Market Timing**: Attempting to predict the top/bottom usually fails. "Time in the market beats timing the market."
- **Home Bias**: Over-investing in your own country's market. Diversify globally (e.g., S&P 500 + Emerging Markets).
- **FOMO**: Investing in "hot" assets (meme stocks, hype-cycles) without a plan.

## Verification Checklist (Investment Health)
- [ ] Portfolio is diversified across at least 3 asset classes.
- [ ] Expense ratios of core funds are below 0.5%.
- [ ] Rebalancing is performed at least once a year.
- [ ] Investment strategy aligns with the goal timeline (e.g., retirement vs. home purchase).
- [ ] Understanding of tax implications (Capital Gains, Dividend Tax).
