# Finance Market Research

## Core Philosophy
1. **Beyond Numbers**: A good researcher doesn't just report data; they synthesize meaning (Insights).
2. **Context Matters**: Analyze assets within their sector and against a relevant benchmark (e.g., SET50, S&P 500).
3. **Risk-Adjusted View**: Always consider volatility and risk metrics (Sharpe Ratio, Beta) alongside returns.

## 1. Fund/Stock Benchmarking
Don't evaluate in a vacuum. Compare performance against:
- **Peer Group**: Similar funds (e.g., Growth funds vs Growth funds).
- **Benchmark Index**: The standard the fund aims to beat.

## 2. Qualitative Synthesis (Inspired by Knowledge Work Plugins)
When summarizing research, follow this structure:
- **The "So What?"**: What does this data mean for the user's goal?
- **Bull Case**: Why would this investment succeed?
- **Bear Case**: What are the primary risks?
- **Recommendation**: Clear, actionable next steps.

## 3. Macroeconomic Analysis
Consider how external factors affect the portfolio:
- **Interest Rates**: Impact on bond yields and stock valuations.
- **Inflation**: Impact on real purchasing power.
- **Geopolitics**: Impact on specific sectors (e.g., Energy, Tech).

## Verification Checklist
- [ ] Research includes a clear comparison against a benchmark.
- [ ] Both Bull and Bear cases are presented.
- [ ] Analysis is tied back to the user's specific financial goals.
- [ ] Data sources are cited where possible.
- [ ] Tone is professional, objective, and insight-driven.
