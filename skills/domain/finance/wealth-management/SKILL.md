# Personal Wealth Management

## Core Philosophy
1. **Financial Awareness**: You cannot manage what you do not measure. Track every asset and liability.
2. **Pay Yourself First**: Automate savings and investments before spending on lifestyle.
3. **Compound Interest**: Time is the most valuable asset. Start early, stay consistent.

## 1. Net Worth Tracking
Your "Financial Scoreboard". Update this quarterly.
- **Assets**: Cash, Savings, Stocks, Real Estate, Business Equity, Retirement Funds.
- **Liabilities**: Credit Card Debt, Personal Loans, Mortgages, Student Loans.
- **Formula**: `Net Worth = Total Assets - Total Liabilities`

## 2. Budgeting: The 50/30/20 Rule
A simple framework for resource allocation:
- **50% Needs**: Housing, utilities, groceries, transport, basic insurance.
- **30% Wants**: Dining out, hobbies, entertainment, travel.
- **20% Financial Goals**: Debt repayment, emergency fund, investments.

## 3. The Emergency Fund
A safety net to prevent selling investments during a crisis.
- **Target**: 3-6 months of **Essential Expenses**.
- **Placement**: High-Yield Savings Account (HYSA) or liquid money market funds.
- **Usage**: Job loss, medical emergencies, urgent home/car repairs.

## 4. Path to Financial Independence (FIRE)
The point where investment income covers all living expenses.
- **FIRE Number**: `Annual Expenses x 25` (based on the 4% Safe Withdrawal Rate).
- **Strategy**: Maximize the gap between income and expenses (Savings Rate).

## Gotchas
- **Lifestyle Creep**: Increasing spending as income rises. Fix this by automating a percentage of every raise into investments.
- **High-Interest Debt**: Debt above 7-8% interest is a "negative investment". Prioritize paying this off before aggressive investing.
- **Inflation**: Cash loses value over time. Wealth building requires assets that outpace inflation (Stocks, Real Estate).

## Verification Checklist (Financial Health)
- [ ] Emergency fund covers at least 3 months of expenses.
- [ ] High-interest debt (e.g., credit cards) is zero.
- [ ] Savings rate is at least 20% of net income.
- [ ] Net Worth is trending upwards over the last 4 quarters.
- [ ] Asset allocation matches current risk tolerance.
